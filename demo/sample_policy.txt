INTERNAL IT SECURITY POLICY
Meridian Technologies Inc.
Document ID: SEC-POL-2026-003
Effective Date: January 15, 2026
Classification: Internal Use Only

1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

This policy establishes the information security requirements for all employees, contractors, and third-party vendors who access Meridian Technologies information systems. It applies to all computing devices, network infrastructure, cloud services, and data repositories owned or operated by Meridian Technologies across all office locations and remote work environments.

2. ASSET CLASSIFICATION

All digital assets must be classified into one of four categories: Public, Internal, Confidential, or Restricted. Asset owners are responsible for assigning classifications within 5 business days of asset creation. Restricted assets require encryption at rest using AES-256 and must be stored exclusively on Meridian-managed infrastructure. Internal assets may be stored on approved cloud platforms provided they meet SOC 2 Type II compliance requirements.

3. ACCESS CONTROL

Access to information systems is governed by the principle of least privilege. All user accounts must be provisioned through the central Identity and Access Management (IAM) system. Privileged accounts (administrator, root, service accounts) require multi-factor authentication using hardware security keys. Access reviews must be conducted quarterly for all systems classified as Confidential or Restricted. Dormant accounts inactive for more than 90 days are automatically suspended.

4. PASSWORD AND AUTHENTICATION REQUIREMENTS

Passwords must be a minimum of 14 characters and must include at least one uppercase letter, one lowercase letter, one digit, and one special character. Password rotation is required every 180 days for standard accounts and every 90 days for privileged accounts. Biometric authentication is permitted as a second factor but must not serve as the sole authentication mechanism. Single sign-on (SSO) via SAML 2.0 or OpenID Connect is mandatory for all SaaS applications.

5. NETWORK SECURITY

All external-facing services must be protected by a Web Application Firewall (WAF). Internal network segmentation must isolate the production environment from development and staging environments. VPN access is required for all remote connections to internal systems, using WireGuard or IPsec with AES-256-GCM encryption. All DNS queries from corporate devices must route through the internal DNS resolver for threat filtering and logging.

6. ENDPOINT SECURITY

All corporate laptops and workstations must run an approved Endpoint Detection and Response (EDR) solution. Operating system patches must be applied within 14 days of release for standard patches and within 48 hours for critical vulnerabilities rated CVSS 9.0 or higher. Removable storage devices (USB drives, external hard drives) are prohibited on systems that process Restricted data. Full-disk encryption using BitLocker (Windows) or FileVault (macOS) is mandatory on all corporate devices.

7. DATA PROTECTION AND ENCRYPTION

All data in transit must be encrypted using TLS 1.2 or higher. TLS 1.0 and 1.1 are explicitly prohibited on all Meridian systems. Database connections must use mutual TLS (mTLS) for service-to-service communication within the production environment. Backup data must be encrypted and stored in a geographically separate region from primary data. Encryption keys must be rotated annually and managed through the centralized Key Management Service (KMS).

8. INCIDENT RESPONSE

All security incidents must be reported to the Security Operations Center (SOC) within 1 hour of detection. The SOC operates 24/7 and maintains a mean time to acknowledge (MTTA) target of 15 minutes. Critical incidents involving data exfiltration or ransomware require immediate escalation to the Chief Information Security Officer (CISO). Post-incident reviews must be completed within 5 business days and findings must be documented in the incident management system. Tabletop exercises simulating major security incidents must be conducted semi-annually.

9. THIRD-PARTY RISK MANAGEMENT

All third-party vendors with access to Meridian data must complete a security assessment questionnaire before onboarding. Vendors processing Restricted data must provide evidence of SOC 2 Type II certification or ISO 27001 compliance. Vendor security posture is reassessed annually. Data processing agreements must specify data residency requirements, breach notification timelines not exceeding 72 hours, and the right to audit. Meridian reserves the right to terminate vendor access immediately upon discovery of a material security deficiency.

10. LOGGING AND MONITORING

All authentication events, privilege escalations, and data access operations must be logged. Logs must be retained for a minimum of 12 months in hot storage and 7 years in cold storage for compliance purposes. Log integrity must be protected through immutable storage or cryptographic chaining. The Security Information and Event Management (SIEM) system must ingest logs from all production systems within 5 minutes of event generation. Automated alerts must be configured for anomalous login patterns, privilege escalation attempts, and data exfiltration indicators.

11. BUSINESS CONTINUITY AND DISASTER RECOVERY

The Recovery Point Objective (RPO) for critical systems is 1 hour and the Recovery Time Objective (RTO) is 4 hours. Disaster recovery plans must be tested annually through full failover exercises. Backup integrity verification must be performed monthly. All critical services must maintain active-active or active-passive redundancy across at least two availability zones. Communication plans must include out-of-band notification channels independent of corporate email.

12. COMPLIANCE AND ENFORCEMENT

Compliance with this policy is mandatory for all personnel. Violations may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment or contract. The Information Security team conducts annual compliance audits. Exceptions to this policy require written approval from the CISO and must include a risk acceptance form, compensating controls, and a remediation timeline not exceeding 12 months.

13. POLICY REVIEW

This policy is reviewed and updated annually by the Information Security Governance Committee. Material changes require approval from the executive leadership team. All employees must acknowledge receipt and understanding of this policy within 30 days of each revision.
